# Kia & Hyundai Vehicle Theft Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes the rise of Kia and Hyundai vehicle thefts across the United States, with a focus on identifying geographic hotspots, temporal trends, and policy-relevant insights.

---

## Objective
To identify high-risk cities and temporal patterns in Kia/Hyundai vehicle thefts in order to support targeted public safety interventions and resource allocation.

---

## Data Sources
- Milwaukee vehicle theft dataset (city-level time series)
- National theft dataset (multi-city comparison)
- Geographic theft dataset (latitude/longitude)
- VICE News dataset (cross-city theft reporting)

---

## Methods

### Data Preparation
- Cleaned and standardized multiple datasets
- Removed inconsistent and non-numeric values
- Parsed and transformed datetime variables

### Data Transformation
- Reshaped datasets from wide to long format
- Aggregated theft counts by city and time
- Created comparative metrics across datasets

### Analysis
- Time-series analysis of theft trends
- City-level comparison of theft volume
- Milwaukee vs national trend comparison
- Geographic distribution analysis

---

## Visualizations
- Stacked area chart (theft trends over time)
- Percentage share of Kia/Hyundai thefts
- Treemap (top cities by theft volume)
- Bar chart (theft breakdown by city)
- Time-series comparison (Milwaukee vs national average)
- Geographic scatter map

---

## Key Insights
- Kia/Hyundai thefts increased sharply starting in 2021  
- Milwaukee shows significantly higher concentration compared to national trends  
- Theft activity is highly concentrated in specific cities  
- Kia/Hyundai thefts represent a growing share of total vehicle thefts
